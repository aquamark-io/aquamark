CACalifornia

Brokers
License and Disclosure required.
Lenders
Disclosure required.
SB-869
CFL license required to broker loans. Disclosure must be signed by the recipient before the transaction is finalized - funding amount, finance charge, total repayment, prepayment policies, APR

CTConnecticut

Brokers
Registration and Disclosure required.
Lenders
Registration and Disclosure required.
SB 1032
Providers and brokers must register with the state. For transactions ≤ $250,000 - must disclose total funding, cost of borrowing, repayment schedule, and broker compensation. APR is not required.

FLFlorida

Brokers
Comply with Broker Code of Conduct.
Lenders
Disclosure required.
HB 1353
Providers must disclose terms for deals ≤ $500,000 - funding amount, finance charge, total repayment, prepayment policies, APR. Brokers must comply with code of conduct: no false adverstising or upfront fees, address and phone number must be included in all advertising.

GAGeorgia

Brokers
No current disclosure requirement.
Lenders
Required to provide disclosures for sales-based financing ≤ $500,000.
HB 392
Georgia focuses on lender disclosure requirements for sales-based financing (like merchant cash advances) under $500,000. The law ensures businesses understand the true cost of revenue-based funding options.

KSKansas

Brokers
No current requirement.
Lenders
Must disclose total financing amount, repayment schedule, and prepayment penalties for deals ≤ $500,000.
HB 2196
Kansas requires commercial lenders to provide transparent disclosures about financing terms, total costs, and repayment schedules for deals under $500,000. This helps small businesses make informed financing decisions.

MOMissouri

Brokers
Must register with the state.
Lenders
Required to disclose repayment terms and total financing cost for deals ≤ $500,000.
SB 894
Missouri requires both broker registration and lender disclosures for commercial financing deals under $500,000. This dual approach ensures accountability from both brokers and lenders in the financing process.

NYNew York

Brokers
Must provide state-mandated disclosure before offer is presented.
Lenders
Must disclose broker compensation, APR, total repayment, fees, and terms for transactions ≤ $2.5 million.
S5470/A7250
New York has comprehensive disclosure laws covering deals up to $2.5 million. Both brokers and lenders must provide detailed information about terms, costs, and compensation to ensure transparency in commercial financing.

NCNorth Carolina

Brokers
Must register with the state.
Lenders
Required to disclose total amount financed, fees, and terms for transactions ≤ $500,000.
HB 334
North Carolina requires broker registration and comprehensive lender disclosures for commercial financing deals under $500,000. The law aims to prevent predatory lending practices in the small business financing market.

UTUtah

Brokers
Must register with the state; disclosures required.
Lenders
Must disclose financing terms and broker compensation for deals ≤ $1 million.
SB 41
Utah requires broker registration and comprehensive disclosures for commercial financing deals up to $1 million. The state focuses on transparency in broker compensation and financing terms to protect small businesses.

VAVirginia

Brokers
Must register; required to issue disclosures for sales-based financing.
Lenders
Must disclose financing terms, fees, and broker compensation.
HB 2091
Virginia requires broker registration and focuses on sales-based financing disclosures. Both brokers and lenders must provide clear information about terms, fees, and compensation to ensure transparency in alternative financing markets.
