State Compliance Requirements

Below is a summary of commercial financing disclosure requirements for brokers and lenders, organized by state.

California

  • Brokers: Must provide specific disclosures to recipients before presenting financing offers.
  • Lenders: Required to furnish detailed financing disclosures, including total cost and APR, for transactions ≤ $500,000.

New York

  • Brokers: Obligated to deliver disclosures outlining financing terms to recipients.
  • Lenders: Must disclose total financing amount, finance charges, APR, and repayment terms for transactions ≤ $2.5 million.

Connecticut

  • Brokers: Required to register with the state and provide disclosures for sales-based financing ≤ $250,000.
  • Lenders: Must disclose total financing amount, fees, repayment terms, and broker compensation for applicable transactions.

Florida

  • Brokers: No specific disclosure requirements outlined.
  • Lenders: Obligated to provide disclosures detailing total financing amount, payment terms, and prepayment rights for transactions ≤ $500,000.

Georgia

  • Brokers: No specific disclosure requirements outlined.
  • Lenders: Must furnish disclosures including total funds provided, payment schedule, and prepayment terms for transactions ≤ $500,000.

Kansas

  • Brokers: No specific disclosure requirements outlined.
  • Lenders: Required to disclose total financing amount, repayment terms, and prepayment costs for transactions ≤ $500,000.

Utah

  • Brokers: Must register with the state and comply with disclosure requirements.
  • Lenders: Obligated to provide disclosures detailing total financing amount, payment terms, and broker compensation for transactions ≤ $1 million.

Virginia

  • Brokers: Required to register with the state and provide disclosures for sales-based financing.
  • Lenders: Must disclose total financing amount, finance charges, repayment terms, and broker compensation for applicable transactions.
