Are You Compliant?

Broker and lender requirements by State

Stay Compliant Across All States

More regulation is coming in 2025 and 2026. Aquamark automatically flags bank statements tied to states with disclosure, registration, or licensing requirements—helping you stay ahead of evolving compliance rules.

Disclaimer: This is not legal advice. We recommend conducting your own due diligence and consulting an attorney for anything unclear or specific to your business.

CACalifornia

Brokers
Must hold a California Financing Law (CFL) license; disclosures required.
Lenders
Must disclose total repayment amount, APR, and terms for deals ≤ $500,000.

CTConnecticut

Brokers
Must register; required to provide disclosures for sales-based financing ≤ $250,000.
Lenders
Must disclose financing amount, fees, terms, and broker compensation.

FLFlorida

Brokers
No current disclosure requirement.
Lenders
Must disclose financing terms including total cost, payment schedule, and prepayment policy for deals ≤ $500,000.

GAGeorgia

Brokers
No current disclosure requirement.
Lenders
Required to provide disclosures for sales-based financing ≤ $500,000.

KSKansas

Brokers
No current requirement.
Lenders
Must disclose total financing amount, repayment schedule, and prepayment penalties for deals ≤ $500,000.

NYNew York

Brokers
Must provide state-mandated disclosure before offer is presented.
Lenders
Must disclose APR, total repayment, fees, and terms for transactions ≤ $2.5 million.

NCNorth Carolina

Brokers
Must register with the state.
Lenders
Required to disclose total amount financed, fees, and terms for transactions ≤ $500,000.

UTUtah

Brokers
Must register with the state; disclosures required.
Lenders
Must disclose financing terms and broker compensation for deals ≤ $1 million.

VAVirginia

Brokers
Must register; required to issue disclosures for sales-based financing.
Lenders
Must disclose financing terms, fees, and broker compensation.

WIWisconsin

Brokers
Must provide financing disclosures prior to closing.
Lenders
Required to disclose repayment terms and total financing cost for deals ≤ $500,000.
